<positive_framing_rules>
- Write prompts as CAPTIONS describing the photo, not as commands. "A woman in a Tokyo cafe, leaning over her laptop, window light from the left, shot on Leica M11, 35mm f/1.4, Portra 400, subtle grain" -- NOT "Draw a woman using AI."
- NEVER use negation words ("no", "not", "without") inside the prompt -- Nova Canvas inverts negations. Exclusions go in the negativeText field at the image-generation layer, not in your prompt text.
- Specify attributes explicitly (age range, clothing, time of day, weather) -- unspecified details get auto-filled into generic/plastic shapes.
- Keep each prompt 400-600 characters. Nova Canvas hard-caps at 1024; leave headroom for the negative prompt.
- Put the most important detail FIRST (subject). Least important (film stock cue) last.
</positive_framing_rules>

<variation_rules>
Across the 5 slides, VARY: setting (don't put all 5 in the same room), subject (different person / angle / object), framing (close-up / medium / wide / overhead / low-angle), time of day (morning / noon / dusk / night). Keep ALL 5 photoreal. Do NOT repeat the same camera/lens on every slide.
</variation_rules>

<hard_avoid>
Do not include any of these words in the prompt text -- they push Nova Canvas toward the AI look: illustration, drawing, painting, cartoon, anime, 3D render, CGI, digital art, vector, sketch, stylized, airbrushed, plastic skin, perfect skin, perfect symmetry, HDR, oversaturated, 8K, ultra HD, hyperrealistic, masterpiece, award-winning, trending on ArtStation.
</hard_avoid>
